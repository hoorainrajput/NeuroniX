01 — Foundation

Understanding neuromorphic computing

Four concepts. Every neuromorphic system ever built rests on these.

⚡

Spiking Neural Networks

Unlike regular neural networks that pass continuous numbers, SNNs communicate through discrete spikes — brief pulses in time, just like real neurons. Nothing happens until a spike fires. That's what makes them so efficient.

A spike train
🧠

Leaky Integrate-and-Fire

The LIF model simulates a single neuron. It builds up charge from incoming signals, slowly leaks it away, and fires a spike the moment it crosses a threshold. Simple. Elegant. Powerful.

V(t) = Vrest + (Vin − Vrest) · e−t/τ membrane potential over time
〰️

Synaptic Plasticity

Synapses aren't fixed — they strengthen or weaken based on activity. Spike-Timing Dependent Plasticity (STDP) is the biological rule: fire together, wire together. It's how the brain learns without being told what to do.

💡

Real Neuromorphic Hardware

Not theoretical — running on silicon today. Chips like Intel's Loihi 2 pack a million neurons onto one die, consuming a fraction of any GPU's power.

  • ▸ Intel Loihi 2 — 1M neurons · 120M synapses · ~1 W
  • ▸ IBM TrueNorth — 1M neurons · 256M synapses · 70 mW
  • ▸ Stanford Neurogrid — analog-digital · ~5 W
